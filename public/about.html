About Me

I have lived and worked in Japan for over 10 years doing jobs that primarily required English and Japanese language skills.
It's perfectly viable for some people who just want to work and live in Japan to just do work teaching English.
Some who develop really good Japanese skills might move on to translation or office worker jobs that want foreigners for some specific role.

In order to expand my skills and break out of this cycle of relying solely on English to find employment, I decided to learn to code.
I've tried learning to code from HTML and had successfully learned the basics going on to CSS and JavaScript.
Unfortunately, I never got to the point of breaking past that wall to get a good-looking portfolio up and finding employment.

It's not easy trying to kickstart a career in Japan in your 30s in an industry you have no previous working experience in, but I'm not backing down.
Despite multiple attempts to try and learn to code, I dug in and left my job to push myself to find a job in the Japan IT industry as I learned to code again.
I'm still inexperienced, but I'm going to prove I can get the job done.
